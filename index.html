About Me

First year Bioinformatics and Systems Biology (BISB) Ph.D. student at Dr. Elizabeth Winzeler's Laboratory from University of California, San Diego. Member of the Malaria Drug Accelerator (MALDA) consortium.

I obtained a B.S. in Computer Science at Autonomous University of Baja California, Mexico and a M.S. in Bioinformatics at The University of Texas at El Paso.
During my MS I constructed a pipeline to get insight into the differential gene expression using Illumina RNA-seq data. My current research, as part of the MalDA team, involves the use of Bio/Chemoinformatics tools to support the work in drug targets and resistance mechanisms identification aiming to discover novel therapeutics for the treatment of malaria.

On my free time I like to read fun books, go to the gym, to the beach, travel and I love camping!
